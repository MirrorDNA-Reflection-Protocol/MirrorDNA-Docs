Symbolic Language

The Glyph System

Glyphs are symbolic compression for human-AI communication. Faster than prose. More precise than emoji. A shared vocabulary for marking meaning.

Why Glyphs

Natural language is ambiguous. Emoji lack semantic precision. Glyphs occupy the middle ground: compact symbols with defined meanings that both humans and AI systems can interpret consistently.

When Claude sees ⟡, it knows: this is an anchor point, a truth claim, something grounded in the vault. When Paul sees △, he knows: decision required, branch in the path.

Glyphs eliminate the prose overhead of "I'd like to note that this is a decision point requiring your input." Just: △

Adoption

Glyphs are not mandatory. They're a tool. Use them when precision and compression matter:

  • Handoffs — Mark key state transitions clearly
  • Decisions — Signal when human judgment is needed
  • Verification — Distinguish fact from estimate from unknown
  • Patterns — Flag when the system recognizes something recurring

Over time, glyphs become natural. You'll find yourself thinking in them.
